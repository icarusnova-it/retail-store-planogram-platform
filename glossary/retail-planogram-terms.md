# Retail Planogram Terms Glossary

## A

**Aisle (Pasillo)**: A major walkway in a retail store, typically running from front to back, containing multiple gondolas on both sides.

**Audit**: Process of verifying planogram execution and compliance with rules, regulations, and guidelines.

**Audit Trail**: Complete record of all planogram operations, including who, what, when, and results.

## B

**Brand Blocking**: Practice of grouping all products of the same brand together within a category.

**Brand Compliance**: Adherence to brand placement guidelines and requirements.

## C

**Category**: Organizational grouping of related products (e.g., Beverages, Dairy, Frozen).

**Category Flow**: Logical arrangement of categories within a store to optimize customer shopping journey.

**Category Management**: Process of managing product categories to optimize sales and customer experience.

**Compliance**: Adherence to rules, regulations, guidelines, and constraints.

**Compliance Score**: Percentage or score indicating level of compliance with rules and requirements.

## D

**Depth**: Number of product units placed front-to-back on a shelf.

## F

**Facing**: Number of product units visible from the front (customer-facing side) of a shelf.

**Facing Optimization**: Process of optimizing the number of facings based on product rotation and sales.

## G

**Gondola (Góndola)**: A freestanding display unit with shelves on both sides, placed in aisles.

## M

**Master Planogram**: Template planogram for a category or product group, not tied to a specific store, used as starting point for store-specific adaptations.

**Module (Módulo)**: One side (left or right) of a gondola, containing multiple shelves.

## P

**Planogram (Planograma)**: Visual representation and data model of how products should be arranged on store shelves and displays.

**Planogram Compliance**: Degree to which actual store execution matches the planogram.

**Planogram Execution**: Process of physically placing products in store according to planogram.

**Planogram Lifecycle**: Complete lifecycle of a planogram from creation through execution, auditing, and optimization.

**Position (Posición)**: Specific location on a shelf where a product is placed, defined by coordinates and dimensions.

**Product Placement**: Act of placing a product at a specific position in a planogram.

**Product Rotation**: Sales velocity of a product, typically categorized as high, medium, or low rotation.

## R

**Rule Engine**: System that executes business rules for automated product placement and validation.

**Rule-Driven Placement**: Automated product placement based on codified business rules.

## S

**Shelf (Nivel)**: A horizontal level within a module where products are placed, numbered from bottom to top.

**Shelf Level**: Number indicating shelf position (1 = bottom, increasing upward). Eye level typically shelf 4-5.

**Store Planogram**: Store-specific planogram adapted from master planogram to fit store characteristics and constraints.

**Subcategory**: Sub-grouping within a category (e.g., Soft Drinks within Beverages).

## V

**Validation**: Process of checking planogram and execution against rules, constraints, and requirements.

**Version Control**: System for tracking planogram versions and changes over time.
