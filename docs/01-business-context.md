# Business Context

## What is Planogramming?

Planogramming (planometr√≠a) is the practice of designing and organizing product displays in retail stores to optimize sales, customer experience, and operational efficiency. In supermarkets and large retail operations, planograms are critical tools for:

- **Visual Merchandising**: Creating appealing product displays
- **Space Optimization**: Maximizing sales per square foot
- **Category Management**: Organizing products by category and subcategory
- **Operational Efficiency**: Facilitating restocking and inventory management
- **Compliance**: Ensuring adherence to brand guidelines and regulations

## The Problem with Current Approaches

### 1. Static Documents (PDFs, Images)

**Current State:**
- Planograms stored as PDF files or images
- No structured data
- Difficult to update and version
- Cannot be queried or analyzed programmatically

**Problems:**
- Manual updates required
- Version control difficult
- No automated validation
- Limited analytics capabilities

**Example**: Planogram stored as PDF. To change product placement, designer must recreate entire PDF.

### 2. Spreadsheets (Excel)

**Current State:**
- Planograms managed in Excel spreadsheets
- Some structure, but limited
- Manual data entry
- Prone to errors

**Problems:**
- No validation rules
- Difficult to maintain consistency
- Limited collaboration
- No integration with other systems

**Example**: Store manager maintains Excel with product positions. Updates manually, errors common.

### 3. Photos and Manual Notes

**Current State:**
- Store execution validated with photos
- Manual notes and checklists
- No digital connection to planogram

**Problems:**
- No systematic tracking
- Difficult to compare across stores
- No analytics
- Time-consuming audits

**Example**: Auditor takes photos of shelves, compares manually with printed planogram.

## The Solution: Data-Driven Planogram Platform

### Key Capabilities

1. **Structured Data Model**
   - Planograms as structured data, not static images
   - Versionable and queryable
   - Programmatic access

2. **Rule-Driven Placement**
   - Automated placement based on rules
   - Constraint validation
   - Exception handling

3. **Store-Specific Variations**
   - Adapt planograms to store characteristics
   - Handle store constraints
   - Support multiple store formats

4. **Operational Execution**
   - Digital execution in stores
   - Real-time validation
   - Photo evidence and checklists

5. **Analytics and Optimization**
   - Compliance metrics
   - Sales impact analysis
   - Continuous optimization
